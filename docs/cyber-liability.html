What Cyber Liability Insurance Is All About:

Cyber Incidents are not just targeted to large multi-national companies today. In the region, particularly in Singapore, we have witnessed the aftereffects of data breaches & the implications to businesses & society.

In the span of 5 years, regardless of the level of IT security processes put in place, government agencies & local SME companies are victims to the series of cyber attacks in Singapore.

  • The Messiah: 2013 Singapore Cyber Attacks (October – December, 2013)
  • MINDEF Cyber Breach (February 1, 2017)
  • WannaCry Ransomware (May 14, 2017)
  • Petya Ransomware (June 28, 2017)
  • K Box Data Breach (September 16, 2014)
  • SingHealth Data Breach (July 4, 2018)

Financial / reputational damages are incurred (in the form of lawsuits and/or public confidence restoration efforts) as a result of data breaches of personal particulars leakages, and both large and small organisations are equally vulnerable to cyber threats in today’s digital world.

The evolution of Cyber insurance is meant to protect businesses from the growing threats in the online space. A comprehensive coverage helps companies in various industries to alleviate the risk of potential financial losses as a result of cyber incidents.

Read on to find out more about this type of coverage, and if it’s right for your business.

What Is Cyber Liability Insurance?

Cyber Insurance coverage can cover a broad range of information security-related claims, including personal data liability, network failures, defence cost, repair of company/personal reputation, etc.

It is often wrongly perceived that cyber incidents are only targeted at large corporations. Hackers or cyber terrorist would aim to steal valuable information (customer or business records) such that they can profit from it. As long as a business is withholding data records of their customers, they are very much at risk.

Regulations such as the 2012 Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA) outlines the framework of how companies should protect customers’ data, and invokes the organisation to strengthen their security processes to mitigate against possible data breaches. Companies which are found to be liable of data breaches could often face severe penalties such as hefty fines or business cession.

The world is just experiencing a beginning phase of cyber threats, and it is very much certain that cyber attacks would be more complex, sophisticated and pervasive than what we are seeing today. Business owners, regardless small, medium or large companies, needs to put in place various security measures to cope with the repercussions of such attacks, and cyber insurance would be an ideal solution.

Do I Need Cyber Liability Insurance?

This type of insurance comes in handy if your business:

  • Has a payment gateway built onto your website
  • Maintains a database of personal information on current, past or prospective customers
  • Stores information on employees digitally, including NRIC numbers or medical information
  • Relies heavily on technology for daily operations
  • Is subjected to PDPA regulations in Singapore
  • Could incur heavy penalties due to business interruptions (i.e. Telco/ electricity/ water service provider)

What Does Cyber Liability Insurance Cover?

Cover is provided on a first party or third party basis, including:

Third Party Cover

Cyber, Data Security and multimedia cover – Compensation to third parties for the failure to protect their information held by the insured.

First Party Cover

Data breach notification cost cover – As a result of a breach of data held by the insured, cover is provided to pay for the expenses incurred or obliged to pay to notify consumers of the breach , legal fees to assist with communications and costs associated with administering the notification process.

Information and communication asset rectification costs cover – Repair, restoration or replacement of the insured’s computer systems where they were damaged, destroyed, altered, orrupted or misused by a hacker.

Regulatory defence and penalty costs cover – Payment related to a regulatory action, penalty or fine (where insurable by law).

Public relations costs cover – Payment incurred for a public relations and crisis management consultant to avert or mitigate any damage to the insured’s brand or operations.

Forensic costs cover – payment for a forensic consultant to identify a hacker, a security specialist to assess electronic security and temporary storage of the insured’s electronic data.

Credit monitoring costs cover – Payment incurred for a credit monitoring service to comply with data breach law.

Cyber business interruption cover – Reimburse for the loss of business income as a result of the interruption, degradation in service or failure of the insured’s computer systems.

Cyber Extortion cover – Payment for the expenses arising from a cyber-extortion threat.

How Much Does Cyber Insurance Cost?

The cost varies based on the requirements of the insured. It is never the case whereby it is a one-size-fits-all policy. However, the premium amount would be greatly dependent on:

  • Company’s turnover
  • Industry type
  • Exposure level
  • Business nature (marketplace? Website only? Payment gateway available?)

Typically, for a SME with < $1,000,000 turnover, it would only cost $1k to cover up to $100,000 limit of indemnity.
